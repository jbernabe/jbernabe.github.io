Questions & Answers

Frequently asked

What motivates your work in climate and space?

Climate change and space weather are among the most consequential challenges of our time‚Äîand they're deeply interconnected. Foundation models give us an unprecedented ability to understand Earth's systems holistically. When we trained Prithvi on NASA satellite data, we saw how AI could detect floods, map wildfires, and monitor crops at scale. With HelioFM, we can now forecast space weather that affects satellites and power grids. The thread connecting all of this work is using open science and open-source AI to make our planet more predictable‚Äîand more resilient.

Why partner with IBM Research?

IBM Research brings a unique combination of deep scientific expertise, massive computational infrastructure, and a commitment to open-source. Our partnerships with NASA and ESA aren't just about publishing papers‚Äîthey're about deploying models that real agencies and governments use for disaster response, climate monitoring, and scientific discovery. We operate research labs across three continents and can bridge the gap between frontier research and real-world impact.

How did the E.ON transformation become an INSEAD case study?

When I joined E.ON as their first CDO, AI was‚Äîin the words of the INSEAD Knowledge article‚Äî"a very abstract concept." The priority was to make it tangible for 70,000 employees. We built Data.ON, a board-sponsored programme encompassing strategy, governance, and operating model. Professor Theodoros Evgeniou from INSEAD recognised the approach as a model for enterprise AI transformation, and we co-authored the case study, which is now taught globally to MBA and executive education students.

What is the intersection of quantum computing and AI?

This is one of the most exciting frontiers in computing. At IBM Research, we're exploring how quantum algorithms can accelerate specific AI workloads‚Äîfrom optimisation problems in logistics and finance to training certain classes of machine learning models. I teach Quantum Applications at LMU Munich, covering quantum optimisation, quantum machine learning, and practical applications. The convergence of quantum and AI isn't science fiction‚Äîit's an active research agenda with real milestones.

How can I collaborate or get in touch?

I'm always interested in conversations around AI for science, quantum computing applications, climate AI, and enterprise transformation. You can reach me through my IBM Research profile, connect on LinkedIn, or find my academic work on Google Scholar and ResearchGate.

Biography

Juan Bernabé-Moreno is a Spanish computer scientist, technology executive, and academic. He is the Director of IBM Research Europe for Ireland and the United Kingdom, where he leads research laboratories in Dublin, Daresbury, and Hursley focused on artificial intelligence, quantum computing, and high-performance computing. He also leads IBM's Algorithms and Applications Research mission for Earth and Space, directing researchers across multiple global research laboratories in partnership with NASA and the European Space Agency (ESA).

Prior to IBM, Bernabé-Moreno served as Chief Data Officer and Global Head of Data and Analytics at E.ON, Europe's largest investor-owned energy company, where he led the company's enterprise-wide data and AI transformation — later documented as a case study by INSEAD business school. He previously held senior data and analytics leadership roles at Telefónica. He is an invited lecturer at the Ludwig Maximilian University of Munich.

Early Life & Education

Bernabé-Moreno was born in Antequera, Málaga, Spain. He studied computer science at the University of Granada, where he obtained his Engineer's degree in 2002, spending part of his undergraduate studies at the Technische Universität Braunschweig in Germany as part of an exchange programme. He later completed a postgraduate master's degree in Soft Computing and Intelligent Systems at the University of Granada (2007–2009) and obtained his PhD in Computer Science from the same institution in 2015, while working full-time in industry.

His doctoral work focused on the intersection of artificial intelligence, natural language processing, and social network analysis, supervised by Professor Enrique Herrera-Viedma. He maintains research collaborations with the Mathematical Institute at the University of Oxford and the AI department at the University of Granada.

Career

Director, IBM Research Europe — Ireland & United Kingdom
2022 — Present
IBM Research  ·  Dublin · Daresbury · Hursley

Bernabé-Moreno joined IBM in June 2022 as Director of IBM Research Europe for Ireland and the United Kingdom. He is responsible for research operations across three laboratories conducting work in artificial intelligence, quantum computing, semiconductors, and other frontier technologies. He engages directly with UK and Irish governments on national quantum and AI strategies, advises policymakers on sovereign computing infrastructure and AI governance, and oversees a joint research programme with Trinity College Dublin.

He leads IBM's partnerships with NASA and the European Space Agency, producing a family of open-source foundation models for science spanning Earth observation, weather forecasting, heliophysics, and multimodal Earth AI. The team also developed the first Earth observation tiny models deployed in space.

Chief Data Officer & Global Head of Data and Analytics
2017 — 2022
E.ON  ·  Munich, Germany

Appointed as E.ON's first Chief Data Officer in April 2017, Bernabé-Moreno was tasked with leading the data and AI transformation of one of Europe's largest energy companies — approximately 70,000 employees across more than 15 European markets. He designed and executed Data.ON, the board-approved enterprise-wide data transformation programme, taking the company from near-zero AI maturity to embedded, scaled AI across the business.

The transformation was documented in the INSEAD business school case study E.ON: Building a New AI-Powered Energy World (Reference 6595), co-authored with Professor Theodoros Evgeniou of INSEAD. The case study is taught to MBA and executive education students globally as a model for enterprise AI transformation. An INSEAD Knowledge article noted that when Bernabé-Moreno joined E.ON, "AI was a very abstract concept" and that his stated priority was to "make it tangible for everyone in the company."

Research & Publications

Bernabé-Moreno has authored over 50 publications in peer-reviewed venues contributing to emergent technology directions. He holds several patents. His early research focused on social network analysis, recommender systems, and natural language processing, while his more recent work spans geospatial AI, quantum computing applications, foundation models, and computational intractability.

Under his leadership, IBM's partnerships with NASA and ESA have produced several widely adopted open-source foundation models for science. The geospatial model Prithvi — trained on NASA's Harmonized Landsat and Sentinel-2 data — supports flood mapping, burn scar detection, and cropland analysis. Prithvi WxC is a 2.3-billion-parameter foundation model for weather and climate forecasting, trained on 160 variables from NASA's MERRA-2 reanalysis data. HelioFM, trained on 14 years of observations from NASA's Solar Dynamics Observatory, supports space weather forecasting. TerraMind, developed with ESA as part of the FAST-EO project, is the first any-to-any generative, multimodal foundation model for Earth observation; its paper was accepted at ICCV 2025.

In November 2025, IBM and ESA released ImpactMesh, a global multimodal dataset for disaster mapping. Bernabé-Moreno is a member of the MIT Climate and Sustainability Consortium (MCSC), representing IBM Research.
